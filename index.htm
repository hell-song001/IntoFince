Elements of investment

Investment

Introduction:

Investment is the commitment of funds for a perion of time with the expectation of receiving more than the current outlay. whether in recurring income or appreciation of price.

Why People Invest:

  1. To earn a return ontheir investment or profit.
  2. To beat inflation.
  3. To save money for a purchase.
  4. To save money for their retiremnet.
  5. To create a form of passive income.

Types of Assets:

Real assets wether tangible like real estate, equipment and machinery or intangible like patents, goodwill, copyright and royalties. and Financial assets.

Financial Assets are those that represent a claim of natural assets or future cash flow arising from it.

It includes:

  • Commercial banks deposits, wether savings or fixed deposits.
  • Insurance policies
  • Equity shares, wether common or prference shares.
  • Derivatives like options and futures.
  • Hybrid securities, like convertibles, warrants and leases
  • Mutual funds and other fund pools like collective investment schemes.

Characteristics of investments:

Retrun
The primary objective of investing. Investors aim to maxmize the return they get from their invetsment.
Risk
A concequence of invetsment that causes a variation in the expected return. Its measured as the standard deviation of the expected returns. The risk depends on the period of the investment. The longer the period the the higher the uncertainty of the retrun, thus the higher the risk.
Safety
Safety of an investment is identified with the ceratinty of return of capital without loss of money or time delay. The more certain an investor will receiveis their return the safer the investment. Depends on the firm.
Liquidity
Liquidity is the ability to easily sell or market an investment without loss or money or time. The easier its to sell and investment the more liquid it is. Demand and supply determine the saleability of an investmentt. Marketability is not neccesary liquidity. .

Thus the main objective of an investent process is to minimize risk while simultenously maxiimizing expected return from the investment and assuring safety and liquidity.

Debt versus Equity:

Debt, is fixed period loan comprising of periodid interest payments and a return of the principal at maturiry.

Equity, represents ownership of a firm. Holders of equity receive dividends and benefits from share price appreciation.

Personal Contracts versus Securities

Personal contracts, comprises of interests and periods of contract that are negotiable between the borrower and the lender. This contacts cannot be transferred to a third party.

Securities are issued in fixed denominations and are fungible as well as tardable.

Risk & Return Framework:

Investment risk, is the probability that the actual return will vary from the expected return

In investing a trade-off must exist. Investors try to earn a higher return while rducing the risks. The risk return framework states that "Return is higher if the risk is also higehr."

The risk-return tradeoff means that an investor will be prepared to accept a higher rsik if the expected return is proportionally higher.
